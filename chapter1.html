The Dewangan Editorial

Chapter One

The Architecture of Service

A chronicle of duty, the weight of ink, and the silent sacrifices that built a legacy.

You have always moved with a steady, quiet resilience. Long before the titles and the responsibilities of the Indian Administrative Service defined your path in the eyes of the world, you were defined by simpler things: the rhythmic cadence of your morning thoughts and the disciplined ink-stained fingers of a lifelong scholar.

I often think of the silence you carry—not a heavy silence, but one of deep contemplation. You taught us that the most significant work isn't always found in the noise of celebration, but in the soft light of a desk lamp at midnight, and the unwavering commitment to a cause greater than oneself.

The Secretariat, 1982. A portrait of quiet dedication.

“Your service was never about the protocol or the rank. It was about the lives touched in the quiet corners of the country, and the integrity you brought to every room you entered.”

As the decades have unfolded into this milestone, your walk has become a dignified stroll, yet the fire of purpose never left your eyes. You have shown us that leadership is not a position to be held, but a grace to be shared with everyone you meet.
